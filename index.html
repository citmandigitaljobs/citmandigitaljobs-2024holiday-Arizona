Flagstaff

Coconino County

After a nine hour train ride, we arrived in Flagstaff an hour or so before dawn. After leaving the station, we crossed Route 66 in the wrong direction and walked in circles for an hour looking for the motel before realising we were on the wrong side of town.

We eventually found our Super 8 motel... with an all you can eat breakfast and more boiled eggs than you could possibly want.

After a sleep, we went for a walk and saw a tumbleweed blowing down a side street!

Off to the real Wild West, to walk in the footsteps of Wyatt Earp and Doc Holliday. After witnessing a shoot-out at the saloon, trying our luck in a rifle range and being entertained by a country and western singer, we made our way back to Tucson... with the first pair of real cowboy boots.

Tucson

Pima County

A day around Tucson, a city which doesn't wake up until 11 am. After trekking in the hot, dry heat to a boot store that turned out to be closed for a few weeks because their air conditioner had broken down, we made our way to 4th Avenue where we enjoyed a delicious Spanish lunch. After browsing the shops, we found Trader Joe's on our way home so we stocked up on simple, fresh food to keep us supplied until we left for Granby.
